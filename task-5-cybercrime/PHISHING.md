
CYBERCRIME CASE STUDY : PHISHING

What the crime is:
Phishing is a cybercrime in which attackers impersonate trusted organizations such as banks, universities, or companies to trick people into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or banking details.

How it  happens :

-A college student named Riya receives an email that appears to be from her bank.
-The email states that her account will be suspended unless she verifies her details immediately.
-It includes a link that looks legitimate but leads to a fake website designed to mimic the bank’s login page.
-Riya enters her username, password, and OTP on the fake site.
-The attacker captures this information and uses it to access her real bank account.
-Within minutes, money is transferred out without her knowledge.

Who is usually targeted:
Phishing often targets students, young professionals, and elderly users. Students are particularly vulnerable because they frequently respond to emails related to internships, scholarships, or banking notifications without verifying their authenticity.

Consequences:
Riya loses a significant amount of money from her account. Additionally, her personal data is compromised, which could be used for future fraud or identity theft. She also experiences stress and anxiety due to the incident. This case shows how a simple mistake clicking an unverified link can lead to serious financial and emotional consequences, highlighting the importance of staying alert online.
